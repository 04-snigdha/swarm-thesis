Hi, the 5000-trial experiment finished running and I've completed the analysis. Wanted to share the results and a follow-up plan before the next meeting.

For the convex shapes, circle came out at 88.1% success and square at 83.9%. Both behave similarly — swarm size drives success, and the shuffle randomness level barely changes anything. The non-convex shapes are more interesting. L-shape is at 62.4%, with structured shuffling (SR=0.0) performing best at 74% and full random (SR=1.0) dropping to 52%. What's notable is that success actually goes down at swarm size 35, which I didn't expect. The C-shape result is the one I want to investigate further — 23% overall, and performance gets worse as swarm size increases beyond 25. Size 35 is worse than size 25, and nothing I varied in shuffle randomness made a clear difference.

I also classified why trials were failing, using the peak number of attached ants as a proxy. About 75% of failures across all shapes were "stuck" failures — meaning ants found and attached to the payload but the collective couldn't finish transport. At small swarm sizes the failures are mostly because ants don't find the object. At larger swarm sizes it flips almost entirely to stuck failures. For C-shape at size 35, it's 196 stuck failures out of roughly 200 total — so the geometry is the problem, not the search.

This made me realise the current experiment design can't properly answer whether shuffling helps non-convex shapes, for two reasons. First, five SR levels is too coarse — the literature on collective transport (specifically a bi-stability model in PLOS CompBio) suggests the optimal noise level is narrow enough to fall between two of my measured points. Second, the shuffle duration was fixed at 2 seconds for everything. Wilson et al. (2014) and Gelblum et al. (PNAS 2016) both point to repositioning duration as the more important variable — and the C-shape degradation pattern I'm seeing is actually what Gelblum's model predicts when there's no emergent oscillation mechanism.

So I'd like to run a targeted follow-up on just L-shape and C-shape, swarm sizes 25–35, with SR swept at 11 levels from 0.0 to 1.0 in steps of 0.1, and shuffle duration varied across 1s, 2s, 4s, and 8s. I'm also increasing the time limit to 240s — successful C-shape trials were averaging 155–163s under the 180s limit, so some of those stuck failures were probably just cut off too early. That gives 13,200 trials total, which I'll run on Colab. Before I start that run, does this plan make sense to you or would you want to adjust anything?
